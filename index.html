About the Project

Green Hydrogen
for Our Community

EcoVolt is a student-led engineering proposal entered into the EEEGR Energy Competition. We are designing a fully integrated green hydrogen production facility — from water intake and electrolysis to compressed storage and a public fuelling station — to demonstrate that clean hydrogen can be produced locally at a community scale.

Core Infrastructure

Four Systems,
One Plant

Our facility integrates four key components working in sequence to produce, store, and distribute green hydrogen with maximum efficiency and minimal environmental impact.

Core
⚡

PEM Electrolyser

A Proton Exchange Membrane electrolyser splits purified water into hydrogen and oxygen using renewable electricity. High efficiency, fast response, compact footprint.

Upstream
💧

Water Treatment Plant

On-site ultrapure water production using reverse osmosis and deionisation. Ensures the PEM stack receives feedwater meeting IEC 62282 specifications.

Storage
🔒

Hydrogen Storage

Compressed gaseous hydrogen stored in high-pressure vessels on site. Buffer capacity decouples production from demand, enabling 24/7 availability.

Offtake
⛽

Fuelling Station

A basic hydrogen refuelling point for fuel cell vehicles and local fleet operators. First-of-kind infrastructure for our local area, enabling regional decarbonisation.

How It Works

From Electrons
to Fuel

01

Renewable Input

Grid or direct renewable electricity powers the facility during periods of surplus generation.

02

Water Treatment

Mains water is purified on-site to ultrapure standard before entering the electrolyser stack.

03

Electrolysis

The PEM stack splits H₂O into hydrogen and oxygen gas. Oxygen is vented; hydrogen is captured.

04

Compression & Storage

Hydrogen is compressed and stored on-site in high-pressure tanks, ready for dispensing.

05

Fuelling

Vehicles and local users access hydrogen at our on-site fuelling station — zero tailpipe emissions.
